About

I am a quantitative researcher based in New Delhi, working at the intersection of machine learning, optimization, and financial markets. My training is in Electrical Engineering, Computer Science, and Operations Research at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Bombay, and over the past several years I have worked as a quantitative strategist in high-frequency trading and as an analyst across fixed income, currencies, and risk — building statistical and machine-learning models that make decisions under uncertainty in live markets.

My research centres on a longer horizon: how data-driven methods can support sustainable, long-term investment decisions. I am drawn to questions of how capital can be allocated responsibly — accounting for environmental, social, and governance (ESG) factors and long-run risk rather than optimizing solely for short-term returns — and to bringing the same rigour I use in markets to these questions: an explicit mechanism, careful data work, honest validation, and clear communication of what the evidence does and does not support.

This site is a research portfolio — a place to show how I frame a question, test it, and communicate the result, drawing on my work in quantitative finance and my earlier research in statistics, machine learning, and information retrieval.

Research Interests

Sustainable & ESG-aware Investing

Incorporating environmental, social, and governance signals into portfolio construction and capital-allocation decisions.

Long-horizon Decision-making

Optimization and decision-making under uncertainty over long investment horizons, balancing return, risk, and externalities.

Machine Learning for Finance

Statistical and deep-learning models for forecasting, signal quality, and robust estimation in noisy financial data.

Information Retrieval & Data Systems

Extracting decision-relevant structure from large textual and market datasets — building on my published work in query expansion and IR.
